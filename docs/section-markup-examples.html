Section 3.2 Examples of Markup

The first example is for solving a linear equation of the form \(mx+b=0\text{.}\) The parameters \(m\) and \(b\) are created randomly.

A second example is calculating derivatives of a simple polynomial of the form \(f(x)=ax^n+b\text{.}\) The parameters \(a\text{,}\) \(b\text{,}\) and \(n\) are created randomly.

A third example illustrates coupled answers and asks to find two functions \(f\) and \(g\) so that \(f(g(x))\) equals a given function.
